About Thyroid Cancer

Thyroid cancer develops from the tissues of the thyroid gland, a small butterfly-shaped gland located in the front of your neck. It plays a crucial role in regulating numerous metabolic processes in the body.

Explore more about thyroid cancer on Cancer.gov.

Types of Thyroid Cancer

  • Papillary Thyroid Cancer: The most common type, accounting for about 80% of cases.
  • Follicular Thyroid Cancer: Accounts for about 10% of cases.
  • Medullary Thyroid Cancer: Develops from C cells in the thyroid.
  • Anaplastic Thyroid Cancer: A rare, aggressive type.

Symptoms

  • A lump or swelling in the neck (goiter)
  • Persistent hoarseness or voice changes
  • Difficulty swallowing or breathing
  • Persistent cough not related to a cold
